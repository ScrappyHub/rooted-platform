# ROOTED — Public Terms of Participation

These terms explain how people may participate in ROOTED in a fair, safe, and respectful way.

ROOTED is a community and civic discovery platform.
It is not a social media network.
It is not a political platform.
It is not an advertising network.

---

## 1. Eligibility

By using ROOTED, you confirm that:

- You are using the platform for lawful purposes.
- You will respect community safety rules.
- You understand that access may vary by role and tier.

---

## 2. Account Behavior

Users may not:

- Attempt to bypass platform safeguards
- Attempt to escalate their own role or privileges
- Harass, threaten, or manipulate others
- Circumvent Kids Mode protections
- Submit false or misleading institutional data

Violations may result in suspension or removal.

---

## 3. Content & Submissions

All public-facing content is subject to:

- Moderation review
- Safety and youth protections
- Community governance rules

No submission is guaranteed publication.

---

## 4. Marketplace Participation

Markets (RFQs, bids, bulk offers, experiences) are:

- Role-gated
- Tier-gated
- Compliance-gated
- Audit-gated

No user is entitled to market access.

---

## 5. Kids Mode

Kids Mode:

- Prohibits monetization
- Prohibits messaging
- Prohibits uploads
- Prohibits persuasion or fear-based content

Violation of Kids Mode safeguards results in permanent restriction.

---

## 6. Platform Availability

ROOTED is provided “as-is” as a community utility.
We do not guarantee:

- Continuous uptime
- Economic outcomes
- Vendor performance
- Institutional outcomes

ROOTED facilitates discovery and coordination, not transactions themselves.

---

## 7. Termination

Accounts may be restricted or removed if:

- Governance rules are violated
- Safety risk is introduced
- Abuse patterns emerge

---

## 8. Changes

These participation terms may evolve only in alignment with ROOTED’s constitutional governance.
No change may weaken youth, consent, or sanctuary protections.

---

ROOTED participation is a privilege, not a right.
Trust and safety always come first.
